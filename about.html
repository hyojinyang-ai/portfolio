What I Believe

Most internal tooling fails not because of bad UI, but because designers never understand the domain deeply enough to question the workflow itself.

I invest heavily in understanding the systems, constraints, and mental models of the people I design for before proposing solutions.

UX maturity isn't about having more designers. It's about embedding design thinking into how organizations make decisions.

My most impactful work has been enabling teams without UX support to apply research practices and design frameworks independently.

The best design leadership is invisible. When teams naturally make user-centered decisions without being told to, you've done your job.

I focus on building systems, rituals, and shared language that outlast any individual project.

AI & Design

I believe AI is fundamentally reshaping how we design, not just what we design. I actively integrate AI into my practice — not as a novelty, but as a force multiplier for research, prototyping, and decision-making.

How I Use AI in My Design Practice

1.

AI-Augmented Prototyping: At King, I used AI tools to generate interactive prototypes in hours rather than weeks, enabling faster validation cycles with game development teams. This shifted conversations from "should we build this?" to "here's what it could feel like — what would you change?"

2.

Research Synthesis at Scale: I leverage AI to analyze interview transcripts, survey data, and support tickets — surfacing patterns across hundreds of data points that would take weeks to identify manually.

3.

Design-to-Code Workflow: I build and iterate on functional prototypes using AI-assisted coding (Claude Code, Cursor, V0.dev), closing the gap between design intent and engineering implementation. This portfolio itself was built using this workflow.

My perspective: AI doesn't replace design judgment — it compresses the feedback loop between idea and validation. The designers who thrive will be those who use AI to spend less time on production and more time on the hard problems: understanding context, navigating ambiguity, and making trade-offs that serve both users and the business.

Leadership & Mentorship

I believe a design leader's greatest impact is measured not by their own output, but by how they multiply the effectiveness of those around them.

Growing Designers

Mentored junior and mid-level designers at H&M, King, and Zalando — helping them develop research skills, stakeholder communication, and design judgment. At H&M, two designers I mentored took on expanded responsibilities within the team.

Building Design Culture

At King, I introduced bi-weekly design critiques and research share-outs across platform teams. These rituals increased cross-team collaboration and created a shared vocabulary for discussing UX quality.

Scaling UX Practices

Created research playbooks, design templates, and decision-making frameworks that enabled product managers and engineers to run discovery independently — reducing dependency on UX support while maintaining quality.

Managing Agency Partnerships

At H&M, led the collaboration between internal design team and external agency (AKQA), balancing workload distribution, maintaining UX consistency, and ensuring agency deliverables met our quality standards.
