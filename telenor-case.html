Telenor - Transforming Telenor's Digital Customer Experience

Senior UX Designer / UX Lead · 2019-2021

Telenor's digital ecosystem was fragmented across legacy and modern platforms, creating inconsistent experiences for customers and limiting the organization's ability to deliver unified, self-service solutions.

The Strategic Challenge

Fragmented Platforms & Inconsistent Experiences

Telenor's digital ecosystem was split across legacy and modern platforms, creating structural inconsistencies that impacted customer trust, increased support costs, and slowed business agility. The organization needed to modernize while maintaining service continuity.

01
Platform Fragmentation - Legacy B2 for Broadband/TV vs. modern Epi for Mobile created inconsistent experiences
02
High Activation Failure - ~1,700 monthly broadband activation failures drove support costs and customer frustration
03
Complex Digital Commerce - Poorly differentiated shopping journeys caused drop-offs and confusion
04
Regulatory Compliance Gap - New legislation required secure digital communication infrastructure

What I led

I worked to unify fragmented customer experiences, reduce friction in critical journeys, and align cross-functional teams around research-driven, scalable solutions.

  • • Led platform modernization from legacy B2 to Epi, creating unified account experiences across mobile, broadband, and TV
  • • Redesigned activation experience to reduce ~1,700 monthly failures and decrease support dependency
  • • Re-imagined digital shopping journey for TV and streaming services with validated concept flows
  • • Designed secure Message Center to meet regulatory requirements and establish centralized digital communication
PART 1

MODERNIZING THE ACCOUNT PLATFORM (B2 → EPI)

CONTEXT
FOCUS
Platform Migration, Account Experience
CHALLENGE
Legacy B2 platform fragmentation
Broadband and TV account pages were hosted on a non-responsive legacy system, inconsistent with mobile experiences, difficult to maintain, and structurally misaligned. Customers lacked clear subscription overview, easy upgrade paths, transparent invoice access, and consistent navigation.
WHAT I DID
Analyzed legacy B2 functionality and user needs
Created user story maps to prioritize migration scope and feature parity
Designed scalable information architecture
Established modular design principles aligned across web and app
Defined iterative roadmap for phased migration
Balanced quick wins with long-term platform consolidation goals
Aligned account structure across all subscription types
Created unified foundation enabling consistent self-service experiences

Impact

  • Unified fixed and mobile account experiences
  • Created foundation for decommissioning legacy systems
  • Enabled scalable feature expansion
  • Strengthened digital self-service capability
PART 2

REDUCING ACTIVATION FAILURE

CONTEXT
FOCUS
Activation Portal Redesign
CHALLENGE
~1,700 monthly broadband activation failures
Root causes included confusing flow, poor error messaging, unclear user segmentation, and structural ambiguity between registration and activation. This created increased support costs, customer frustration, and churn risk.
WHAT I DID
Facilitated design sprint with cross-functional team
Mapped activation journey, identified breakdown points, and prioritized structural fixes
Prototyped mobile-first activation flows
Conducted usability testing focused on completion confidence and error understanding
Redesigned information hierarchy and branching logic
Simplified user segmentation, improved error and progress feedback
Transformed technical portal into guided journey
Shifted from feature-focused UI to trust-building onboarding experience

Impact

  • Reduced friction in high-stakes onboarding moment
  • Created opportunity for ~1M SEK annual support cost savings
  • Increased activation clarity and customer confidence
  • Supported platform modernization roadmap
PART 3

RE-IMAGINING THE DIGITAL SHOPPING JOURNEY

CONTEXT
FOCUS
TV & Streaming Commerce
CHALLENGE
Complex, inconsistent shopping experience
The digital shopping journey was complex and inconsistent, poorly differentiated between TV and streaming, causing drop-offs in sensitive steps, and not aligned with in-store experience. Customers wanted simplicity, personalization, control, and transparency.
WHAT I DID
Mapped entire digital commerce journey
Identified friction points across package selection, comparison, and checkout
Facilitated cross-functional ideation workshops
Developed two validated concept flows: wizard-based guided journey and single-page filter-based overview
Introduced early BankID personalization logic
Simplified package comparison and add-on selection based on customer context
Proposed structured A/B experimentation framework
Enabled data-driven optimization of commerce flows

Impact

  • Improved clarity in package differentiation
  • Strengthened digital-first commerce strategy
  • Reduced cognitive load in checkout
  • Enabled scalable experimentation roadmap
PART 4

DESIGNING SECURE DIGITAL COMMUNICATION

CONTEXT
FOCUS
Message Center, Compliance
CHALLENGE
New legislation requiring encrypted communication
The organization lacked unified message history, secure digital inbox, structured notification logic, and clear ownership. New legislation required encrypted communication for personal data, creating regulatory pressure.
WHAT I DID
Conducted user interviews and survey (428 responses)
Mapped communication types and channel expectations across customer segments
Facilitated design sprint with business, legal, and tech
Aligned cross-functional teams on secure Message Center concept and roadmap
Designed secure Message Center concept
Defined MVP vs long-term roadmap with clear prioritization criteria
Structured 1-way → 2-way communication evolution
Created phased approach balancing compliance, user needs, and technical feasibility

Impact

  • Achieved legal compliance readiness
  • Created centralized digital message experience
  • Improved customer trust and control
  • Established roadmap for two-way communication

Key Impact

Reduced activation friction

Improved digital self-service foundation

Enabled legacy decommissioning

Increased clarity in commerce flows

Prepared platform for secure digital communication

Strengthened UX maturity within organization

Key Takeaways

At Telenor, UX was not about polishing interfaces — it was about reshaping systems and creating clarity within complex, constraint-heavy environments.

The work required balancing customer needs with technical limitations, regulatory requirements, and organizational realities. By leading with research and facilitating cross-functional alignment, I helped turn fragmented digital touchpoints into scalable, self-service experiences that supported both customer confidence and business outcomes.

  • → Unified disconnected platforms into coherent account and commerce journeys
  • → Reduced operational cost by improving activation and self-service flows
  • → Enabled platform modernization without disrupting live services
  • → Established research-informed decision-making across teams

The foundations, patterns, and practices introduced during this period continue to influence Telenor’s digital product development.
