H&M: Scaling the Global Membership Program to 100 Million Users

Lead UX Designer · 2015-2019

Led UX strategy and design teams to overhaul H&M's global membership program, directly contributing to doubling the active member base from 50 million to 100 million across 74 markets in just one year.

The Strategic Challenge

A Disconnected Experience Was Stifling Growth

H&M's existing digital experience was fragmented. The "My H&M" account and the "Club" loyalty program were treated as separate products, leading to significant customer confusion and a disconnect between the brand's digital and physical stores.

My role: Lead the 'My account & Membership program' UX team, which included two UX designers and an external agency (AKQA). I was responsible for unifying the customer experience, defining the strategic direction with stakeholders, and ensuring our design solutions delivered on key business outcomes.

Stagnant Member Growth

The confusing sign-up process, especially in-store, was a major barrier to acquiring new members

Low Engagement & Perceived Value

Customers struggled to understand the benefits of membership

Lack of Data & Insight

Without a unified view of the customer, strategic decisions were difficult

From Fragmentation to a Unified, Omni-Channel Vision

01

Simplifying Sign-Up

We identified the in-store sign-up as the biggest bottleneck. Through workshops with stakeholders and developers, we designed a flexible, quick sign-up form that could be adapted for market-specific needs, such as WeChat integration in China.

User Pain Point: "I was triggered by the 10% when paying, but it took a long time to sign up and it was so embarrassing when the line got longer behind me"

Solution: Simple, quick & flexible sign-up form with market-specific flexibility

02

Unifying the Experience

A core part of our strategy was merging the "My H&M" account with the "H&M Club" into a single, intuitive entity. This involved extensive user research and concept validation to ensure the new structure met customer expectations.

User Pain Point: "My H&M and Club are similar to me and I would love to have all this in My H&M. All of my information about me."

Solution: Connect My H&M and H&M Club to deliver more relevant and personal features and offers

My account (H&M Member)

  • Defined the problem: Existing members confused by inconsistent structure across touchpoints
  • Championed this effort to the steering group, arguing that a unified account was vital to long-term customer relationship
  • Managed delegation to UX designers while ensuring consistent user experience
03

Clarifying Member Value

To combat the perception that membership had no benefits, we introduced a tiered system ("Member" and "Plus Member") with gamification elements. I managed the collaboration between our internal team and external agency to develop this concept.

User Pain Point: "What's in it? I don't see any benefit of becoming an H&M Club member."

Solution: Introduce tier levels with gamification elements (unlock and collect points) and provide clear overview of membership benefits

Membership page where we explain what's the membership and benefit of each tier level

  • Balanced UX work between internal employees and external agency (AKQA)
  • Conducted guerilla tests and user tests of membership concepts

04

Creating a Seamless Omni-Channel Journey

We tackled major friction points between online and offline shopping, introducing digital receipts, omni-channel offers, and "Pay Later" service that worked seamlessly in-store and online.

4.1. Omni-channel offers without redeeming points

Problem: "It's just a discount as other discounts you have, why I should redeem my points to get it?" & "I shop online because I don't really have the time to go out, but why I can't get this offer for my online order"

Solution: More relevant offers that H&M members can use without redeeming points regardless of online or offline channels

Omni-channel vouchers can be found on My page and checkout

My Role: Shared customer insights, crafted concept, ran user tests, and maintained UX balance after handover

4.2. Digital Receipt

Problem: "I have to have my paper receipt with me to make a return to the store which I lost."

Solution: Customers can get store receipts digitally in their account, making it easier to track purchases and make returns without paper

Digital Receipt, Store receipt is found in the purchase history together with the online orders

My Role: Crafted concept, ran user tests, led UX design approach through multiple iterations to balance technical feasibility

4.3. Pay Later service regardless of channel

Problem: "It's annoying to take my phone out to show my Club card and credit card to pay for my items (in Store)"

Solution: Remove thresholds by using a QR code in-store with the same payment method online

Key Design Decisions & Trade-offs

We chose a flexible, market-adaptive sign-up over a single global form.

A universal form would have been simpler to build, but user research across markets revealed that sign-up requirements varied dramatically (WeChat in China, BankID in Sweden, email-only in others). The trade-off was higher development complexity, but it unlocked market-specific conversion optimization that a one-size-fits-all approach would have blocked.

We merged "My H&M" and "H&M Club" despite strong internal resistance to combining separate P&Ls.

The account team and loyalty team operated as separate business units with different KPIs. Research showed customers saw no distinction. I championed the merger to the steering group, arguing that customer confusion was costing more than organizational convenience. The trade-off was a 6-month realignment of internal metrics, but the unified experience drove measurably higher engagement.

We deprioritized gamification depth in favor of clear value communication.

Early concepts included complex gamification mechanics (challenges, streaks, badges). User testing revealed that customers first needed to understand basic membership value before engaging with gamification. We simplified to a two-tier system (Member / Plus Member) with clear benefit visualization, deferring deeper gamification to later iterations.

Navigating Organizational Complexity

The hardest part of this project wasn't the design — it was aligning a fragmented organization around a unified customer vision across 74 markets with different technical stacks, regulatory requirements, and business priorities.

As Lead UX Designer, I:

  • Influenced the steering group to prioritize customer experience over organizational convenience, securing approval for the My H&M / Club merger
  • Managed a team of two UX designers plus an external agency (AKQA), distributing work based on expertise while maintaining UX consistency across all deliverables
  • Established user research as a decision-making input — before my involvement, product decisions were primarily driven by business stakeholders without systematic customer input
  • Navigated conflicting priorities between the global team (wanting standardization) and market teams (wanting localization), finding a modular approach that satisfied both

Key Takeaway

The greatest challenge was shaping a unified vision that balanced conflicting customer behaviors, business needs, and deep technical constraints across 74 markets. My key role was to foster a culture of collaboration, bringing stakeholders, designers, and developers together toward a common goal.

By consistently sharing customer insights, we shifted the organization's mindset from assumption-based ideation to data-driven problem-solving. This project succeeded because we built a shared understanding and a collective drive to deliver a truly customer-centric experience.

Outcomes & Next Steps

What changed: customers could better understand membership value and navigate a more coherent account journey.

What scaled: modular, market-adaptive UX patterns enabled global expansion without losing local relevance.

Next: continue improving post-signup retention loops and deepen personalization around loyalty behavior.
