STRATEGIC UX LEADERSHIP

DRIVING STRATEGIC UX DESIGN & MATURITY AT KING

CONTEXT
ROLE
Principal UX Designer, Core Platform
PRODUCT
UP Portal, Workspace Management, Environment Manager
TIME PERIOD
September 2022 – Present
TEAM
PMs, Engineers, Tech Leads, Game Teams, UX Designers
King's internal platform organization faced significant UX challenges: fragmented tooling, inconsistent experiences across domains, and low UX maturity. Internal users—game developers, operators, and technical teams—struggled with steep learning curves, poor discoverability, and workflows that required extensive manual coordination.
ACHIEVEMENTS
Defined and operationalized a shared UX vision
Established clear UX principles and a design framework adopted across Core Platform teams
Elevated UX maturity across multiple teams
Introduced research protocols, design rituals, and systematic documentation practices
Embedded research and UX practices into product development
Created templates, playbooks, and frameworks that enabled teams to run discovery independently
Increased UX visibility in cross-functional decision-making
Championed UX strategy at leadership level, securing buy-in for user-centered development
STRATEGIC UX LEADERSHIP

DEFINING & OPERATIONALIZING A UX VISION

CONTEXT
FOCUS
UX Vision, Principles, Leadership Alignment
CHALLENGE
No shared understanding of UX quality or direction
Teams across King's Core Platform operated without a unified UX framework. This led to inconsistent experiences, duplicated efforts, and difficulty scaling quality across products. Leadership recognized the need for strategic UX direction but lacked a clear, actionable framework.
ACHIEVEMENTS
Created a shared UX vision aligned with product and tech
Facilitated workshops with leadership to define aspirational future state and strategic goals
Defined five UX pillars adopted across teams
Effortless • Self-explanatory • Personalized & Adaptable • Unified & Seamless • Self-service & Autonomy
Secured leadership buy-in and active sponsorship
Presented vision at leadership forums, resulting in organizational commitment to UX investment
Enabled teams without UX support to apply the framework
Created practical guidelines and decision-making tools that teams could use independently
UX RESEARCH LEADERSHIP

ESTABLISHING A UX RESEARCH & INSIGHT LOOP

CONTEXT
FOCUS
Research Culture, Insight Infrastructure
CHALLENGE
Solution-first mindset, no systematic research practice
Teams operated with limited user insight, relying on assumptions and anecdotal feedback. There was no centralized research repository, no standard research protocols, and limited capacity to validate solutions with users before development. This resulted in missed opportunities and rework.
ACHIEVEMENTS
Built research protocols and templates
Created repeatable frameworks for interviews, usability testing, and insight synthesis
Created a centralized research repository
Established Dovetail as the single source of truth for all user research and insights
Enabled teams to run research independently
Trained PMs and designers on research methods, reducing dependency on UX support
Connected insights to OKRs and planning
Integrated research findings into quarterly planning cycles, influencing product roadmaps
PRODUCT EXPERIENCE LEADERSHIP

SCALING SELF-SERVICE WORKSPACE MANAGEMENT

CONTEXT
FOCUS
IAM, Workspace Management, Self-Service
USER GROUP
Workspace Owners, Game Developers
Workspace owners struggled to manage access, permissions, and team structures within King's internal platform. Complex IAM flows required manual coordination with support teams, creating bottlenecks and frustration. Poor visibility into workspace state led to errors and security risks.
ACHIEVEMENTS
Led end-to-end UX strategy for workspace & IAM flows
Defined user journeys, pain points, and opportunity areas through discovery research
Reduced manual coordination and support dependency
Designed self-service flows that enabled workspace owners to manage access independently
Delivered measurable improvements in efficiency and satisfaction
Reduced workspace setup time from hours to minutes, increased user confidence
Established scalable self-service patterns
Created reusable design patterns applied across other Core Platform products
METRIC BEFORE AFTER CHANGE
Workspace Setup Time 2-4 hours 5-10 minutes ~90% reduction
Support Tickets (Workspace) ~50 tickets/month ~15 tickets/month ~70% decrease
User Satisfaction (CSAT) 52 points 87 points +35 points
Self-Service Adoption ~20% of tasks ~80% of tasks 4x increase
PRODUCT EXPERIENCE LEADERSHIP

SIMPLIFYING ENVIRONMENT MANAGEMENT

CONTEXT
FOCUS
Environment Conflicts, Development Workflow
USER GROUP
Game Developers, QA, Tech Leads
Game teams experienced frequent environment conflicts, unclear environment states, and slow development cycles. Developers struggled to understand which environments were available, how to configure them, and how to troubleshoot issues. This created friction and slowed time-to-production.
ACHIEVEMENTS
Led deep discovery across job families
Conducted interviews and workflow analysis with developers, QA, and operations teams
Defined flexible environment capabilities
Designed a system that balanced simplicity for common cases with power for advanced users
Translated research into clear requirements
Created detailed specs and user stories that aligned engineering and product
Ensured consistency across platform UX
Applied established design patterns and interaction models from other platform products
STRATEGIC INITIATIVE

GAME SPACE — A CENTRALIZED PLATFORM EXPERIENCE

CONTEXT
FOCUS
Platform Consolidation, Discoverability
SCOPE
Strategic concept, validation, MVP definition
King's internal platform consisted of dozens of disconnected tools, each with separate authentication, navigation, and mental models. Users struggled to discover capabilities, wasted time context-switching, and faced a steep learning curve. Leadership recognized the need for a unified platform experience but lacked a clear vision.
ACHIEVEMENTS
Proposed and validated the Game Space concept
Created vision prototypes showing a unified, game-centric platform experience
Led co-creation and validation workshops
Facilitated sessions with game teams to refine concept and validate value proposition
Defined MVP scope and product requirements
Prioritized features based on user needs, technical feasibility, and business impact
Secured buy-in for future development
Presented strategic case to leadership, resulting in allocation of resources for Game Space MVP
