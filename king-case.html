RECENT WORK

Streamlining the Fiction Editor Update Experience

CONTEXT
🎮 Internal Game Engine Ecosystem
ROLE
UX Lead / Product Designer
PRODUCT
Fiction Editor, Fiction Launcher
TIME PERIOD
2025–2026 (Strategic Initiative)

The internal game engine ecosystem suffered from low adoption and fragmented workflows. Only 30–50% of users were on the latest editor version, meaning critical hotfixes and new features took months to reach production teams.

Despite having a launcher tool, the update experience was broken: users bypassed the official launcher, version mismatch errors were common, updates felt risky and disruptive, and manual version tracking via Slack and Jira was required.

The underlying issue wasn't just tooling — it was trust, predictability, and system coherence.

The mission: Create a Cohesive Content Creation Ecosystem where creators are always just "One click away from their work."

The Core Problems

Fragmentation & Low Adoption

~55% of users bypassed the launcher and downloaded builds directly. This created version fragmentation and delayed adoption of fixes.

Update Anxiety

Users feared that updating would "break the build." As a result, they hoarded old versions or delayed updates defensively.

"Updates feel risky and unclear and require manual actions." — User feedback

Manual Overhead

Version tracking relied on Slack and Jira. Incompatible launches caused project de-syncs and merge conflicts.

Strategic Alignment & Approach

As the UX lead, I initiated the discovery phase by conducting qualitative research with developers, artists, and technical artists. I synthesized the findings and shared insights broadly to build a shared understanding of why this initiative mattered — not just what needed to be fixed.

I facilitated a series of cross-functional workshops to prioritize the most critical problem to solve first, define clear problem statements, align UX, engineering, and product around shared objectives, and identify technical enablers required for sustainable change.

We defined a guiding vision: "One click away from my work."

Unified & Seamless
Updates should extend workflows without breaking them
Effortless

Design Solution: Smart Project Launch System

After identifying the highest-impact problem, I led ideation sessions to generate solution concepts. We explored and refined ideas using AI-generated prototypes to quickly visualize flows and test assumptions, then prioritized features and defined a focused MVP.

1. Smart Project Launch

  • Double-clicking a project file automatically launches the correct editor version
  • Version mismatch errors are eliminated
  • Manual version management is removed

2. Structured Update Journey

Mapped the update experience into: Awareness → Decision → Locate → Action → Launch

  • Awareness: Clear "What's New" messaging with severity labels (Hotfix, Minor Update, Major Update)
  • Decision Support: "Team-Tested" badges, risk labels, and severity indicators to reduce anxiety
View Prototype

3. Non-Blocking Updates

  • Updates download silently in the background
  • Users can continue working
  • Installation no longer blocks productivity

4. Continuity & Trust

Workspace layouts, panels, and shortcuts persist across updates. Preserving context was essential to rebuilding trust in the update system.

Validation & MVP Refinement

Once the MVP direction was defined, I planned and facilitated validation sessions with users to understand perception of update safety, clarity of communication, confidence in automated version management, and understanding of risk labels and team governance. Feedback was incorporated iteratively, refining both feature scope and messaging before implementation.

Key Takeaway

This initiative demonstrates how UX leadership can reframe fragmented tooling as a systemic workflow issue, align teams around a shared understanding of risk and trust, drive both architectural and experiential change, and transform manual, anxiety-driven processes into predictable, scalable systems. By grounding the work in research, aligning stakeholders early, and shaping the MVP through iterative validation, we turned version management from a source of friction into a strategic advantage.
